Getting there

Fly, don't float

Nonstop flights from Philadelphia to Nantucket Memorial (American / JetBlue) take about an hour and a half. Round trips have recently run $330–440 per person, trending higher in peak late August — the ledger carries $700–1,000 for the two of us.

We looked hard at a romantic all-rail route (Amtrak, the CapeFLYER train, fast ferry). Verdict: ~10 hours each way to save maybe $200. Flying wins.

Caveat: the small planes into ACK are famously fog-prone. Book morning flights, keep expectations flexible.

Choose one · where we stay

Old whale money, creaky floors

The goal: a place that feels like Nantucket — not a resort that could be anywhere. Tap a candidate to put it in the ledger. Nightly figures are peak-season estimates including tax.

  • The classic

    Jared Coffin House

    An 1845 brick mansion from the island's whaling heyday — antique furnishings, old-world creak, steps from the Whaling Museum. Often the best value of the character hotels.

    est. $450–650 / night
  • The romantic

    Union Street Inn

    A tiny, top-rated inn in a 1770s house downtown. Quaint rooms, celebrated cooked breakfast, walk-everywhere location. Books up fast.

    est. $600–800 / night
  • The full experience

    The Nantucket Hotel & Resort

    Bigger and livelier, with pools and a guests-only whale watching charter run by a born-and-raised island captain.

    est. $650–900 / night
Choose one · getting around

Two wheels, whole island

Nantucket is 14 miles long with ~35 miles of paved paths running separate from the roads. We'd rent from Young's Bicycle Shop (family-run since 1931, right at the wharf). Prices below are per person, per day.

  • Paths & town

    Cruiser / hybrid

    Comfy 8-speeds with racks for beach gear. Perfect for the 'Sconset loop and Madaket sunset ride.

    est. $35–45 / day each
  • Paths + the moors

    Trail bikes

    Cannondales with front suspension and trail geometry — unlocks the sandy doubletrack of the Middle Moors and Altar Rock, the best riding on the island.

    est. $45–60 / day each
  • Maximum range

    E-bikes

    Bosch pedal-assist, 65-mile range, both ends of the island in a day. Overkill for us, but listed for honesty. 2-day minimum in peak season.

    est. $90–110 / day each

Fine print: Main Street is lumpy historic cobblestone — everyone walks their bikes a block — and the ocean headwind home from Madaket is a rite of passage.

The itinerary anchors

Whales, past and present

  • Locked in · $20 each

    The Whaling Museum

    A restored 1847 candle factory with a 46-foot sperm whale skeleton overhead, the story of the whaleship Essex (the real-life inspiration for Moby-Dick), and a rooftop deck over the harbor.

  • If dates align · free to watch

    Race Week & the Opera House Cup

    Aug 12–16: nine days of regattas ending with a parade of all-wooden classic boats. After the 16th the island mellows — same weather, thinner crowds.
